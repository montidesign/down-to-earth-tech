## What Are the Primary Duties of Computer network support specialists?

Computer network support specialists are responsible for a vast range of tasks. Depending on your company, these may vary from day to day. A typical day for a tech support expert can include the following activities:

- Putting new technologies, programs, and software together
- Maintaining current gear and software regularly
- System and application troubleshooting
- Providing support to other workers or clients of the business
- Replacing faulty or damaged components or hardware
- Creating hardware and software status reports
- Running software diagnostics
- Ensuring all systems are secure
- Testing for electrical safety

## Soft Skills To Look For In Computer Network Support Specialists

Aside from the technical talents you want your computer network support specialists to have, a few soft skills are required for the position. Soft talents are unquantifiable skills that benefit any firm but are only sometimes simple to recognize because they do not appear on paper.

These are as follows:

### Adaptability and Versatility

Adaptability and versatility are essential in every tech employment since these occupations demand the ability to adjust to changing conditions, technology, and issue-solving.

### Analytic Thinking

Computer network support specialists are issue solvers with strong analytical abilities and attention to detail. As a result, they must have analytical skills and attention to detail to troubleshoot or work backward from an issue.

### Communication

Computer network support specialists often assist people who may have a different level of experience or understanding than they do. Explaining a problem or what went wrong to someone is part of the job. It is usually advantageous to express technical knowledge simply and effectively.

## Common Interview Questions and Skill Evaluations

When looking for computer network support specialists, several questions may be asked. It will often rely on the position of the tech support professional in your firm and whether they will be working with other workers or employees. It will also depend on whether you want them to be highly specialized in a specific sort of system or whether you require them to be more well-rounded.

Here are a few sample questions we gathered that you might find useful:

### How do you troubleshoot problems?

![The computer network support specialists you would hire should be versatile in their IT work. ](https://downtoearthtech.net/media/IT-Network-Support-Specialists.jpg)

You could learn a lot from this question since it discloses the candidate's problem-solving abilities and approach, as well as an indication of their expertise.

Request that they address a sample network problem. Make up a scenario and ask the applicant how they would troubleshoot or try to solve the given problem.

### How confident are you in your capacity to communicate complex IT concerns to the average person?

Your potential computer network support specialists must explain complex issues to clients or employees. It is critical to understand that the necessary communication abilities are there.

### Can you describe subnetting to someone who doesn't have a technical background?

This is a question 3 example since it provides the candidate with a precise topic to explain.

### What are the differences between a hub, a router, and a switch?

You'll want to learn more about their technical skills and how they comprehend these topics.
